Memes

In her work Memes in Digital Culture Limor Shifman explores what it means to be a meme in the digital platform. Specifically, in her chapter "When Memes Go Digital", she talks about a term that she coins as "hypermemetic logic". Basically, Shifman means that memes, much like Dawkin's original use of the word implies, copy and spread faster than ever before, but their "evolution as a new vernacular that permeates many spheres of digital and non digital expression" (23). Specifically, Shifman explores the movement of certain memes and the ways they are reproduced and shared or injected back into the group, community, or internet.

Shifman's attention to the ways in which memes are reproduced, reworked, and shared back into the community are useful for this project because she refers to memes and their ability to replicate, share ideas, and forge new groups as a kind of new internet vernacular. Since memes are so heavily based on images, it is not a far leap to include emojis into this "new internet vernacular".
