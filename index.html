Emojis As A New Language

The aim of this project is to display the way that emojis act as a new vernacular of response in text messages and on the internet. Specifically, Marshall McLuhan, in his work "Laws of Media", described different aspects of technologies when considered as extensions of the human body. If, as McLuhan postulates, technologies are extensions of the human body that enable mankind to extend his reach into virtually anything, then texting can be placed on the table for consideration.

My theory is that texting, as a technology of extension, so far ourstripped the expectations humans had about internet communication, that objects vital to communication, such as facial expression and tone of voice, were lost. In McLuhan's terms, facial expressions and tone of voice are things which were "obsolesced" by the new technology. However, I would like to argue, through this project, that the inclusion of emojis into the phenomenon of texting has retrieved what was obsolesced. I would also like to argue that the inclusion of emojis is becoming a retrieval of earlier forms of communication, like pictographic language.
