Writing Restructures Consciousness

Walter Ong, in his work "Writing Restructures Consciousness", echoes McLuhan's sentiment that there is a distancing that occurs when an extension of human ability is created and enacted. For Ong, this extension is writing. "Technologies," he states, "are not mere exterior aids but also interior transformations of consciousness" (81). For Ong, the extension enhances the consciousness of humans. His description of the effects of extension are more philosophical than McLuhan's, yet they are saying the same thing.

Ong's main focus is that of how writing restructures the thinking and reasoning of humans. He charts the history of language from pictographs to the creation of the alphabet to its modern use. Of special concern to this project is Ong's trail of thinking which leads him to state that writing creates an "autonomous discourse...a discourse which cannot be directly questioned or contested as oral speech can because written discourse has been detached from its author" (77). Ong's writing directly echoes McLuhan's theory of tools of extension. In this case, writing is the tool. Writing detaches itself from the writer, extending their consciousness into the universe, and obsolescing the need for speech.

For this project, Ong's work with writing as an extension directly relates to the notion that texting is an extension of the human self. When orality was the way information and knowledge were passed from one being to another, the speech was linked directly to the speaker. The invention of writing allowed the author's words to stay on the page, and in existence, for much longer than ordinary speech. This allowed an author a degree of extension, of voice and knowledge and information, that was not available to them before. Texting, in the opinion of this project, does the same work, yet adds speed and obsolesces facial expression and body language.
