Extension and Reversal

"All of man's artefacts...are extensions of the physical human body or the mind. Man the tool-making animal has long been engaged in extending one or another of his sense organs in such a manner as to disturb all of his other senses and faculties."

In his article "Laws of Media", Marshall McLuhan talks about technologies as extensions of the body - as in a hammer or clothing are equal parts suppression and extension of man's ability to drive a nail into a board and to control his body temperature (94). Mainly, McLuhan is concerned with the effects these extensions have upon human communication. The extensions that McLuhan refers to create what he calls a "new service environment" and "deeply modifies the very nature and image of people who use it" (96). "The transformational effects of our artificial organs - they generate totally new conditions of environmental service and of life - these are the concerns of Laws of Media" (97).

These are the laws that McLuhan states as being representative of Media:

  • What does the artefact enhance or intensify or make possible or accelerate?
  • What is pushed aside or obsolesced by the new 'organ'?
  • What recurrence or retrieval of earlier actions and services is brought into play simultaneously by the new form?
  • When pushed to the limits of its potential, the new form will tend to reverse what had been its original characeristics. What is the reversal potential of the new form?

McLuhan was heavily concerned with the way technologies influenced the body and what humans were able and not able to produce because of those extensions.
